Molecular Biology of SARS-CoV-2


General Information


On December 12th of 2019, patients from the city of Wuhan reported experiencing novel pneunomia-like symptoms which hospitals struggled to ameloriate with standard therapeutics at the time. Upon initial investigation into the source of this illness, it was reported that each case was linked the Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market. Cases of this ilness began to increase exponentially, prompting immediate investigation into elucidating the genome of this virus. On January 10th of 2020 the data was uploaded to a genomic database, GenBank which served as foundation for further scientific study (Source: CDC).



SARS-CoV-2 was identified as causative agent of the COVID-19 pandemic, ushering scientists to engage in remarkable structural studies of the virus using advanced techniques such as Cryogenic Electron Microscopy (Cryo-EM) to investigate how exactly SARS-CoV-2 infects living cells. This page aims to introduce its readers to the structural biology of COVID-19, explaining how the SARS-CoV-2 enters host cells to initiate its replication cycle.
